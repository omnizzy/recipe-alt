Nigerian Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a vibrant, smoky, and delicious one-pot rice dish cooked in a rich tomato and pepper sauce. It's a staple at Nigerian parties, gatherings, and family meals across West Africa.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 45 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 large tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 onion (divided)
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 seasoning cubes (e.g., Maggi)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups chicken or beef stock

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, and half of the onion into a smooth mixture.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot and sauté chopped onions until translucent. Add tomato paste and fry for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Pour in the blended tomato mixture. Cook on medium heat for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil rises to the top.
  4. Add curry powder, thyme, seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir well and add the stock.
  5. Rinse rice until the water runs clear. Add to the pot and stir. Cover and cook on low heat for 30 minutes.
  6. Stir occasionally and add a bit of water if needed. Cook until rice is soft and flavorful.

Tips

  • Tip: Use foil or parchment paper to trap steam inside the pot for softer, well-cooked rice.
  • Note: For a smoky flavor, let the rice slightly toast at the bottom—just don’t burn it!
